Project Information

In my first year of Uni at the University of Otago I mentioned to one of my friends in South Africa that I can code and I am also interested in Web Devlopment.I was then tasked with the developing a website for my friend whom at the time resided in South Africa. My friend Konke the founder of the business Fudel a eccomerce business where customers would buy groceries online, collect them at one of our warehouses or have them delivered to their doorstep.

Skill Set utilized

  1. Javascript, Html & CSS

    I put all my knowledge to the test, and relearning what I have learnt which have solidified my knowledge of Javascript, hmtl and css.I learnt how to be more efficient in debugging as I know what each line of code does, making the debugging process more efficient.


  2. UX/UI design

    Having web design in mind, designing for scalability and user-centered design.

Key Contributions

  1. Increased marketing capabilities

    Through prompting customers to save their emails I increased the companies marketing capabilities through automated emails that we sent weekly with discounts on products we have recently started selling.


  2. Personalized shopping

    Through monitoring our customers shopping patterns, I created a "For you" page which would have products that would resonate with that particular customer, and when the company can we have them at a discount to increase customer retention.


  3. Efficient business operations

    Before the site was built the company had an issue of allott of general enquiries, now since I added a Faqs page, and an enquiries chat bot most general enquiries are answered by the chatbot. This lets the company focus on ensuring orders are recieved to customers.

Key Learning Points for me.

  1. The concept of designing for scalabilty

    • write code with scalability in mind, writing readable code and including comments which point to relevant code in css, the backend-code(Javascript, python, java, etc) and html.

    • the hompage should include several files that can be eaily replaced as the homepage gets edited the most.



  2. Designing for Usability

    • intuitive design

    • use consistent naming conversion (obviously aha!!)

    • avoid linking the user out of the section.
